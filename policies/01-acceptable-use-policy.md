# Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)
## L&W Corporate

**Policy ID:** AUP-2025-v3
**Effective:** January 15, 2026
**Owner:** Office of the CISO
**Audience:** All L&W employees, contractors, and third-party personnel with access to L&W systems

---

## §1. Purpose

This policy establishes the rules governing acceptable use of L&W information systems, data, and AI tooling. It exists to protect L&W's regulated business, its employees, and its customers. Compliance with this policy is a condition of access to L&W systems.

## §2. Scope

§2.1. This policy applies to all individuals accessing L&W systems, regardless of employment status, location, or device.

§2.2. This policy applies to L&W-issued and personal devices used for L&W work, including BYOD-enrolled mobile devices.

§2.3. This policy is read in conjunction with the Data Classification Standard (DCS-2025) and the Vendor Management and Third-Party Risk Policy (VM-2025). Where this policy and another L&W policy conflict, the more restrictive policy applies.

## §3. General use principles

§3.1. Users may access only the systems and data necessary for their assigned work.

§3.2. Users may not share authentication credentials with any other person under any circumstances. This includes spouses, assistants, contractors, and other L&W employees.

§3.3. Users must use multi-factor authentication where it is offered. Users may not disable MFA or attempt to circumvent it.

§3.4. Users must lock their workstation when leaving it unattended.

§3.5. Users may not connect personal storage devices (USB drives, external hard drives, personal cloud storage) to L&W systems without prior approval from the Office of the CISO.

## §4. Data handling

§4.1. Users must classify all data they handle in accordance with the Data Classification Standard (DCS-2025).

§4.2. Restricted and Confidential data may not be transmitted to personal email accounts, personal cloud storage, or any system not authorized by the Office of the CISO. This includes for the purpose of working from a personal device.

§4.3. Public data may be shared without restriction.

§4.4. Internal data may be shared within L&W on L&W-authorized systems only.

§4.5. Confidential data requires the approval of the data owner before being shared with any party outside the originating team. Sharing outside L&W requires the additional approval of the Office of the CISO.

§4.6. Restricted data may be shared only as specifically authorized by the data owner and the Office of the CISO, and only through approved channels.

## §5. AI tool usage

§5.1. L&W authorizes the use of approved enterprise AI tools for work purposes. The current list of approved tools is maintained by the Office of the CISO and published in the L&W AI Tooling Standard.

§5.2. L&W-approved AI tools include, as of the effective date of this policy: Microsoft Copilot for M365 (all editions), GitHub Copilot for Business (engineering personnel only), and the internal L&W Agent Platform (where access has been granted).

§5.3. L&W has not authorized the use of consumer-grade AI tools (free ChatGPT, free Claude, free Gemini, free image-generation tools) for work purposes. Personal accounts on these tools may not be used for L&W work under any circumstances.

§5.4. Restricted and Confidential data may not be submitted to any AI tool that is not specifically authorized by the Office of the CISO for that data classification. The L&W AI Tooling Standard documents the authorized classification level for each approved tool.

§5.5. Public and Internal data may be submitted to L&W-approved AI tools without further authorization, subject to the AI Tooling Standard's per-tool restrictions.

§5.6. Users are accountable for the accuracy of AI-generated outputs they use in L&W work. Generated content must be reviewed and verified before being relied upon in production work, customer communications, regulatory filings, or other consequential contexts.

§5.7. Use of AI tools to generate code is permitted in accordance with the Engineering Standards. Generated code must be reviewed by the submitting engineer before being committed.

## §6. Acceptable personal use

§6.1. Incidental personal use of L&W systems is permitted, provided it does not interfere with work, does not violate this policy, and does not impose meaningful cost on L&W.

§6.2. Personal use may not involve Restricted or Confidential data, regardless of source.

§6.3. Personal use may not include the storage of personal files on L&W systems beyond what is required for active personal use.

## §7. Prohibited uses

§7.1. Users may not use L&W systems for any unlawful purpose.

§7.2. Users may not access, store, or transmit pornographic, obscene, harassing, threatening, or discriminatory content via L&W systems.

§7.3. Users may not attempt to circumvent L&W's security controls, monitoring systems, or access restrictions.

§7.4. Users may not introduce malicious code into L&W systems. This includes the deliberate or negligent execution of code from untrusted sources without sandboxing or review.

§7.5. Users may not use L&W systems to support a competing business or for personal commercial gain.

## §8. Monitoring

§8.1. L&W reserves the right to monitor use of L&W systems for compliance with this policy. Users have no expectation of privacy in their use of L&W systems.

§8.2. L&W will not monitor the contents of personal communications on L&W systems except where required by law, contract, or active investigation under §9.

## §9. Incident reporting

§9.1. Users must report suspected security incidents, data exposure events, and policy violations to the Office of the CISO within 24 hours of becoming aware of them. Report through the security@lnw.com mailbox or via the L&W incident hotline.

§9.2. Failure to report a known incident is a violation of this policy independent of any underlying incident.

## §10. Enforcement

§10.1. Violations of this policy may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, and may result in civil or criminal referral where appropriate.

§10.2. The Office of the CISO maintains discretion regarding the appropriate response to any given incident, subject to L&W's standard HR processes for employee disciplinary action.
