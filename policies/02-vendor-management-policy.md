# Vendor Management and Third-Party Risk Policy
## L&W Corporate

**Policy ID:** VM-2025-v2
**Effective:** April 1, 2026
**Owner:** Chief Procurement Office, in partnership with the Office of the CISO and Legal
**Audience:** All L&W employees engaging external vendors, service providers, or contractors

---

## §1. Purpose

This policy establishes L&W's vendor engagement standards, including the diligence required before engaging a vendor, the contractual protections required, the ongoing risk management expected during the engagement, and the offboarding obligations when an engagement ends.

## §2. Scope

§2.1. This policy applies to all third-party engagements that involve (a) the exchange of L&W data, (b) access to L&W systems, (c) operation on L&W's behalf in a regulated capacity, or (d) annual L&W spend exceeding $25,000.

§2.2. Engagements below all of those thresholds (small-dollar one-time vendors with no data exchange) may follow the streamlined procurement track defined in the Procurement Standard.

§2.3. This policy supersedes prior vendor management policies. Existing vendor relationships are brought into compliance at the next contract renewal or by the effective date of this policy, whichever is later.

## §3. Vendor classification

§3.1. Every vendor engaged under this policy is classified by tier:

§3.2. **Tier 1 (Critical).** Vendors whose failure would result in immediate material impact to L&W's business operations, customer commitments, or regulatory standing. Includes cloud infrastructure providers, core gaming-platform vendors, and primary financial services partners.

§3.3. **Tier 2 (Significant).** Vendors whose failure would impair L&W's operations meaningfully but not immediately. Includes major software vendors, key consulting partners, and significant data providers.

§3.4. **Tier 3 (Standard).** Vendors whose engagement falls under this policy but does not rise to Tier 1 or Tier 2 impact.

§3.5. Classification is performed by the Chief Procurement Office at engagement start and reviewed annually. Classification may be adjusted based on changes in the engagement scope or risk profile.

## §4. Pre-engagement diligence

§4.1. All vendors under this policy must complete L&W's standard Vendor Risk Assessment (VRA) before engagement.

§4.2. Tier 1 vendors require additionally: a SOC 2 Type II report (or equivalent), independent financial-health review, and an executive-level reference check with at least two comparable customers.

§4.3. Tier 2 vendors require additionally: a SOC 2 Type I report (or equivalent) and a financial-health review.

§4.4. Tier 3 vendors require the VRA only, unless specific risk factors (data sensitivity, regulatory exposure) warrant additional review.

§4.5. Diligence findings are reviewed by the Office of the CISO (for security risk), Legal (for contractual risk), and the engagement sponsor before the engagement is authorized.

## §5. Contractual protections

§5.1. All vendors under this policy must enter a written agreement that includes, at minimum: scope of work, data handling provisions consistent with the Data Classification Standard, security requirements appropriate to the vendor's tier, indemnification and limitation of liability, audit rights, and termination provisions.

§5.2. Tier 1 vendors require additionally: a Service Level Agreement with measurable SLOs, executive escalation paths, and a tested disaster recovery commitment.

§5.3. Tier 2 vendors require an SLA with measurable performance commitments.

§5.4. All vendor agreements must be reviewed by Legal before execution. Engagement sponsors may not enter binding commitments on L&W's behalf without Legal review.

## §6. Ongoing risk management

§6.1. Tier 1 vendors are reviewed quarterly. Reviews include SLA performance, security posture, financial-health updates, and changes in the regulatory environment.

§6.2. Tier 2 vendors are reviewed semi-annually.

§6.3. Tier 3 vendors are reviewed annually at contract renewal.

§6.4. Engagement sponsors are responsible for raising material vendor concerns to the Chief Procurement Office as they arise, regardless of the standing review cadence.

## §7. Data handling

§7.1. Vendor access to L&W data is governed by the Data Classification Standard (DCS-2025). Vendors may access only data necessary to perform the agreed scope of work.

§7.2. Vendor access to Restricted or Confidential data requires the explicit approval of the data owner and the Office of the CISO, in addition to the standard procurement approvals.

§7.3. Vendors must demonstrate appropriate technical and organizational controls for the classification of data they handle. The minimum controls per classification level are documented in the Data Classification Standard.

## §8. AI vendor specific provisions

§8.1. Vendors offering AI products or services must additionally disclose: the models used, the training-data provenance (to the extent the vendor can disclose), data-retention practices for inputs and outputs, and any model-output monitoring practices that involve L&W data.

§8.2. AI vendors may not use L&W data to train models that serve other customers without explicit contractual permission. This restriction is non-negotiable for Tier 1 and Tier 2 AI vendors.

§8.3. The Office of the CISO maintains the list of approved AI vendors and the data-classification level authorized for each.

## §9. Offboarding

§9.1. At the end of every vendor engagement, the engagement sponsor must verify that L&W data has been returned or destroyed in accordance with the contract, that vendor access to L&W systems has been revoked, and that any L&W-issued credentials have been deactivated.

§9.2. Offboarding for Tier 1 and Tier 2 vendors is documented in the Vendor Lifecycle Management system. Tier 3 offboarding is documented at the engagement sponsor's discretion but the data return / destruction and access revocation steps are mandatory regardless of tier.

## §10. Exceptions

§10.1. Exceptions to this policy require the written approval of the Chief Procurement Office and the Office of the CISO. Exceptions are time-bound and reviewed at the expiration of the approval period.

## §11. Enforcement

§11.1. Engagement of a vendor outside the requirements of this policy may result in disciplinary action and may invalidate the engagement.

§11.2. The Chief Procurement Office maintains a quarterly compliance report and surfaces material non-compliance to the relevant business leader.
