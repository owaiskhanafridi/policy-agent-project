# Travel and Expense Policy
## L&W Corporate

**Policy ID:** TE-2025-v4
**Effective:** February 1, 2026
**Owner:** Finance / Controller's Office
**Audience:** All L&W employees and authorized contractors incurring travel or business expenses

---

## §1. Purpose

This policy establishes L&W's expectations for business travel and expense reimbursement, and the controls applicable to expense submission and approval.

## §2. Scope

§2.1. This policy applies to all L&W employees and authorized contractors who travel on L&W business or incur reimbursable business expenses.

§2.2. Personal travel or personal expenses are out of scope. Mixed-purpose travel is addressed in §6.

## §3. General principles

§3.1. **Travel only when necessary.** Business should be conducted virtually where reasonable. L&W's investment in collaboration tooling exists precisely to reduce unnecessary travel.

§3.2. **Spend as if it were your own money.** Reimbursement is not a benefit; it is a reconciliation of expenses incurred on L&W's behalf. The standard is reasonable and necessary.

§3.3. **Document everything.** Receipts are required for all reimbursable expenses except where explicitly noted in §7.

## §4. Approval

§4.1. Domestic business travel requires the approval of the traveler's direct manager.

§4.2. International travel requires the approval of the traveler's direct manager and the traveler's VP.

§4.3. Travel exceeding $5,000 in estimated cost requires the additional approval of the relevant business-unit head.

§4.4. Approvals are obtained before booking, not after the fact.

## §5. Air travel

§5.1. Domestic flights of four hours or less are booked in coach.

§5.2. Domestic flights longer than four hours and all international flights may be booked in premium economy or economy plus. Business class is permitted for international flights of eight hours or more.

§5.3. Bookings are made through the L&W corporate travel platform. Direct bookings outside the platform require prior approval.

§5.4. Standard L&W policy is to book the lowest reasonable fare. Reasonable factors include arrival time, layover length, and traveler safety. Lowest possible fare is not required if it imposes meaningful productivity or safety cost.

## §6. Mixed-purpose travel

§6.1. Travelers may combine personal travel with business travel. L&W reimburses only the business-related incremental cost.

§6.2. The traveler is responsible for documenting the business-incremental cost. Where the business portion cannot be cleanly separated, the traveler errs on the side of personal classification.

## §7. Meals

§7.1. **Travel meals.** L&W reimburses reasonable meal expenses during business travel. The daily meal cap is $100 in the continental US, $125 in high-cost-of-living US cities (NYC, SF, Boston, Seattle, DC), and the published GSA per-diem rate plus 25% in international destinations.

§7.2. **Business meals with external parties.** L&W reimburses business meals with customers, partners, candidates, and other external parties. The cap is $150 per attendee. Meals exceeding the cap require the approval of the host's VP and a written justification.

§7.3. **Business meals between L&W employees.** Reimbursement requires a clear business purpose documented at submission. Meals lacking business purpose are not reimbursable. The cap is $75 per attendee.

§7.4. **Client entertainment.** Including client dinners, drinks, sporting events, and other client-hosted activities. The cap is $250 per attendee. Above-cap entertainment requires the approval of the host's VP plus the business-unit head and a written justification including specific business purpose.

§7.5. **Receipts.** Receipts are required for meals over $25. Itemized receipts (showing line items, not just totals) are required for meals over $75.

§7.6. **Alcohol.** Alcohol is reimbursable when consumed in the course of a business meal. Standalone alcohol purchases are not reimbursable. Alcohol-only social events with employees are not reimbursable.

## §8. Lodging

§8.1. L&W reimburses standard business-class accommodations. Premium tier accommodations are not reimbursable without prior approval.

§8.2. Travelers may use loyalty programs and accumulate personal benefits. L&W does not require return of personal loyalty rewards.

§8.3. The lodging cap is $300 per night in the continental US, $400 in high-cost-of-living US cities, and the published GSA per-diem rate plus 25% in international destinations.

## §9. Ground transportation

§9.1. Rideshare and taxi for business-related ground transportation is reimbursable.

§9.2. Rental cars are reimbursable in mid-size or standard class. Upgrades are not reimbursable without prior approval.

§9.3. Personal vehicle use is reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage rate. Tolls and parking are separately reimbursable.

## §10. Conferences and training

§10.1. Conference and training registration fees are reimbursable with manager approval.

§10.2. Conference travel follows the air, lodging, and meal provisions of this policy.

§10.3. The per-employee annual professional-development cap is $5,000 inclusive of registration, travel, lodging, and meals. Above-cap requests require VP approval.

## §11. Expense submission

§11.1. Expenses must be submitted within 30 days of being incurred. Expenses older than 60 days are not reimbursable except in documented unusual circumstances.

§11.2. Submission is through the L&W expense platform. Manual submissions outside the platform are not accepted.

§11.3. Submitters certify expense accuracy and business purpose at submission. False or padded submissions are grounds for discipline.

## §12. Personal expenses incurred during business travel

§12.1. Travelers may incur reasonable personal expenses during business travel (toiletries, laundry on multi-day trips, etc.). These are reimbursable to a daily incidental cap of $25.

§12.2. Personal entertainment (movies, sightseeing, personal shopping) is not reimbursable regardless of business travel context.

## §13. Enforcement

§13.1. Material non-compliance is referred to the Controller's Office and may result in disciplinary action including non-reimbursement of submitted expenses.

§13.2. Fraudulent submission is grounds for immediate termination.
